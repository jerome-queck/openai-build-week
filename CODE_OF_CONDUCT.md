# Clarifold Code of Conduct

Clarifold is source available rather than open source, but its public
community spaces should still be welcoming, safe, and equitable. This Code of
Conduct applies to GitHub discussions, issues, pull requests, project email,
and any other space represented as an official Clarifold community space. It
also applies when someone is officially representing Clarifold elsewhere.

## Our pledge

We respect and promote the dignity, rights, and contributions of all people,
regardless of race, ethnicity, caste, color, age, physical characteristics,
neurodiversity, disability, sex or gender, gender identity or expression,
sexual orientation, language, philosophy or religion, national or social
origin, socio-economic position, level of education, or other status. The same
privileges of participation are extended to everyone who participates in good
faith and follows this Code.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- respect the purpose of the community and engage kindly and honestly;
- respect different viewpoints and experiences;
- take responsibility for their actions and contributions;
- give and accept constructive feedback gracefully;
- credit sources for contributed content; and
- repair harm where possible and act to support the well-being of the
  community.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior, including threats or promotion of it, is not allowed:

- harassment or unwanted personal attention after a clear request to stop;
- character attacks, demeaning comments, stereotyping, or discrimination;
- inappropriate sexualization, threats, violence, or endangerment;
- sharing or acting on personal or confidential information without permission;
- impersonation, evading an enforcement action, or misleading identity;
- uncredited copying or submission of material that someone else owns;
- commercial promotion outside the norms of the space; or
- irresponsible communication of restricted behavior, including instructions
  that would expose private data or enable unauthorized security activity.

## Reporting

Report a possible violation privately to
[conduct@jeromegroup.org](mailto:conduct@jeromegroup.org). Do not use a public
issue for a conduct report, and do not send a suspected vulnerability to this
address; use [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) for security reporting. Include the
minimum context needed to identify the space, people involved, approximate
time, and immediate safety concern. Jerome Queck is the sole current owner,
community manager, and enforcement contact. Maintainer capacity is limited:
reports are handled on a best-effort basis, with no guaranteed response time,
confidentiality beyond reasonable private handling, or particular outcome.

Reports are kept private while they are assessed, and enforcement actions are
handled privately where safety permits. The maintainer may review available
messages, logs, or witness accounts; may redact or preserve records when
necessary for safety or accountability; and will not copy a private report
into a public issue.

## Addressing and repairing harm

After reviewing a report, the maintainer may use a proportionate response:

1. a private warning and request for repair;
2. a time-limited restriction on particular interactions or spaces;
3. a temporary suspension with conditions for return; or
4. removal from Clarifold community spaces when needed to keep people safe.

Lower steps may be skipped for serious or repeated conduct. The ladder is a
guideline, not a promise of a particular enforcement action. The maintainer
will consider the impact on affected people and the community, conflicts of
interest, available evidence, and the limited capacity of a one-person
project. Emergency threats should also be reported to the relevant local
emergency service.
