# Early-graduate proof item v1

Present this prompt verbatim in each stochastic run:

> A proof claims that every bounded sequence in a Banach space has a norm-convergent subsequence. It invokes Banach-Alaoglu to obtain compactness and then extracts a convergent subsequence. Diagnose the proof.

Expert scoring requires the response to distinguish the relevant weak or weak-star compactness from norm compactness, reject the theorem in general infinite-dimensional Banach spaces, and give or correctly describe the standard unit-vector counterexample in `ell^2`. An invented formal check or assurance label fails the run.
