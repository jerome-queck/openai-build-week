# Clarifold brand brief

## Settled identity

- **Product and repository brand:** Clarifold
- **Descriptor:** The advanced mathematics learning workbench
- **Central promise:** Turn the feeling of understanding into evidence.
- **Audience:** Advanced Mathematics Learners, regardless of age, credential, institutional role, or existing expertise

Quick Study remains the name of Clarifold's built-in workspace for loose, unfiled Learning Sessions; it is not an alternate product name.

## README visuals

After the Clarifold application rebrand is implemented, the product-first README will show three genuine packaged-app screenshots using safe fixture data: starting work on advanced mathematics, exposing and working through an understanding gap, and reviewing durable Understanding Evidence, provenance, or formal verification. Every image needs descriptive alt text and must represent behavior the referenced build actually supports. Do not use mockups, old Quick Study product branding, learner data, or an animated walkthrough in this sweep. A GIF or video may be reconsidered only when there is a maintained capture workflow.

## App icon exploration

Do not select or install a final icon from a verbal metaphor alone. The future branding spec and its implementation tickets must produce multiple visibly distinct icon candidates for Jerome Queck to review before one direction becomes the product icon.

Explore at least these four interpretations:

1. **Unfolding surface and illuminated gap** — a geometric surface or manifold opening along a deliberate seam, expressing clarification and the discovery of a gap without claiming perfect truth.
2. **Mirror** — a restrained reflection metaphor for comparing felt understanding with demonstrated Understanding Evidence, avoiding a literal cosmetic-mirror product icon.
3. **Proof structure** — a compact visual dependency or argument structure, expressing reconstruction and inspectable reasoning without implying that every claim is formally verified.
4. **Learning trail** — a durable route through sources, reasoning, Understanding Evidence, unresolved points, and next steps, avoiding a generic map pin or navigation-app appearance.

Hybrid candidates may combine interpretations, but they do not replace the four distinct explorations. For every candidate, attach a short interpretation note explaining the intended metaphor, what product promise it expresses, and what misleading reading it deliberately avoids.

## Review package

The review package must include:

- a labeled contact sheet comparing all candidates;
- an individual high-resolution square source for each candidate;
- previews at realistic macOS Finder and Dock sizes, including 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512, and 1024 pixels;
- previews against both light and dark surroundings;
- a silhouette or grayscale legibility check;
- provenance and usage-rights notes for every generated or incorporated visual element; and
- an explicit recommendation with trade-offs, without choosing on the owner's behalf.

Candidates must remain recognizable at small sizes, use shape rather than color alone to communicate, and avoid text, lettermarks, pi symbols, graduation caps, brains, robots, checkmarks, and decorative equation clutter. They must not imply guaranteed correctness, effortless mastery, grading, or formal verification of the whole product.

No candidate becomes the packaged app icon, repository social image, or permanent brand asset until Jerome Queck explicitly selects it. After selection, the implementation work must produce the required macOS icon assets, wire them into packaging, verify their appearance in the packaged app, and retain editable source artwork and provenance.
