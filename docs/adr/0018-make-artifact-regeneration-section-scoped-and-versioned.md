# Make artifact regeneration section-scoped and versioned

Consolidated Learning Artifacts will remain directly editable. Each material edit or regeneration creates a recoverable Artifact Revision with its provenance rather than overwriting the only durable result. The learner may inspect or restore prior revisions without exposing the underlying agent transcript as the primary interface.

Agent regeneration will default to Section Regeneration. It changes only the learner-selected section while preserving Required Trail Items, verbatim Personal Notes, and learner-authored content outside that boundary. Replacing the entire artifact remains an explicit separate action rather than the consequence of asking for one local improvement.

Verification evidence remains associated with the exact claims and Artifact Revisions it examined. A semantic change marks only the affected claim's prior evidence as Changed since check, returns the current claim to Not independently checked unless other Current evidence applies, and offers rechecking. Unchanged claims retain their Current evidence, and formatting-only edits do not force mathematical reverification.
