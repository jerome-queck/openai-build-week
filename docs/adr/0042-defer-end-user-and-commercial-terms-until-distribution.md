# Defer end-user and commercial terms until distribution

Clarifold will not add an end-user licence agreement, terms of service, commercial customer contract, subscription or payment terms, data-processing agreement, or generic warranty and support commitments during the current source-available development phase. Clarifold has no hosted account service, payment flow, institutional deployment, or signed and notarized installer for ordinary users, so speculative terms would create overlapping legal authorities and maintenance obligations without a real transaction or data flow to govern. The exact PolyForm Noncommercial software license remains unmodified.

The first signed public installer, paid licence or commercial permission, hosted account, synchronisation or learner-data service, institutional deployment, or App Store distribution triggers a mandatory legal and distribution review before that capability is offered. The review must reconsider the needed end-user and service terms, payment and cancellation terms, data-processing obligations, privacy disclosures, governing law and dispute handling, warranties, liability allocation, support commitments, consumer-protection requirements, and how software and Clarifold brand permissions relate.

At that trigger, the responsible agent must explicitly bring the checklist and current product facts back to Jerome Queck for a fresh decision-by-decision review. It must not generate or accept boilerplate terms, infer consent from this deferral, or carry forward current assumptions after the product, ownership entity, distribution channel, or jurisdictions have changed. Professional Singapore-appropriate legal review is a decision to make with Jerome at that point, not a present requirement.
