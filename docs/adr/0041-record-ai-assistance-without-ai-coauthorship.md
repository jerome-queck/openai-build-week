# Record AI assistance without AI co-authorship

Clarifold will treat AI systems as disclosed tools rather than human or legal co-authors. The human maintainer or approved collaborator remains the commit author and accountable reviewer. When an AI system materially contributes, the final commit records neutral provenance such as `Assisted-by: Codex <model>` and, when a genuine session reference is available, `Codex-Session: codex://threads/<thread-id>`. Equivalent authentic tool-generated provenance may be retained for other agents, but session identifiers must never be invented or duplicated. The OpenAI Build Week `Codex-Feedback-Session` trailer is removed, and AI systems no longer receive `Co-authored-by` trailers.

The pull request must describe the material AI assistance and the human verification applied to it. AI provenance never replaces contributor intellectual-property terms, source and dependency review, security triage, tests, mathematical review, documentation-impact declaration, or ordinary protected-branch controls. A session reference is an audit aid, not evidence that output is correct, secure, original, or legally safe.

Private learner material, credentials, and unreleased confidential commercial material must not be submitted to an AI service unless that service and data flow have been explicitly approved for the material. Every human contributor remains responsible for checking generated material for copied content, incompatible licensing, sensitive data, unsafe behavior, and truthful attribution before it enters Clarifold. The repository's agent and contribution documentation must share one canonical version of this policy rather than retaining tool- or event-specific duplicates.
