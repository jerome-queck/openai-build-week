# Minimize data sent for external research

Automatic web-based Source Corroboration is available under every Session Access Policy. It will use Derived Research Queries containing only the theorem names, assumptions, and mathematical keywords needed to locate suitable evidence. The active policy limits which local material may inform a query, but Focused and Workspace Access do not disable the web-verification capability. The app will not silently transmit raw passages, local paths, filenames, annotations, or other identifying workspace context to an external research service. A Learning Session may grant explicit Research Egress Permission for raw excerpts when they are genuinely needed; that permission must remain inspectable and revocable.

An app-wide Source Excerpt Egress Preference, disabled by default, may remove repeated excerpt-level confirmations. When enabled, an agent may transmit only relevant excerpts, equations, or selected pages from sources already accessible under the active Session Access Policy. The exact material and destination remain inspectable, and the permission can be restricted for an individual session. It never authorizes automatic whole-file transmission, broader filesystem access, local paths, unrelated pages, annotations, or Personal Notes. Sending a complete local file remains a separately confirmed action.

External research and model inference are separate data boundaries. Research Egress Permission does not authorize transmission to a model provider, and choosing a model provider does not authorize raw source uploads to research services. The product must explain these boundaries without suggesting that local-first storage means all computation is offline.
