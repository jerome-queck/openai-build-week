# Publish a factual privacy disclosure

Clarifold will publish a plain-language root `PRIVACY.md`, linked prominently from the product-first README and the app's About Clarifold surface. It must distinguish local storage from model inference, external research, and other network boundaries; explain what may leave the learner's Mac; document the governed treatment of source excerpts and Personal Notes; identify local deletion and export controls; state the current absence of a Clarifold account system, hosted learner-data storage, advertising, and product analytics only while those statements remain true; identify applicable third-party terms; and warn against placing private learner material or credentials in public reports. Privacy questions, corrections, deletion questions, export questions, and suspected mishandling of learner data go privately to `privacy@jeromegroup.org`; suspected security vulnerabilities remain on the separate security-reporting path. The disclosure must carry a last-updated date and change whenever product data flows or third-party processors change. It is a factual data-practices disclosure, not a claim that all computation is offline and not a substitute for future jurisdiction-specific legal review if Clarifold's data practices or commercial footprint materially expand.

Clarifold's present report-handling procedures must also cover personal data received through privacy, security, conduct, licensing, and issue channels: collect only what the report requires, restrict private-channel access to Jerome Queck, prevent private reports from being copied into public issues, define proportionate retention and secure deletion, and keep a private incident record. `privacy@jeromegroup.org` is the public privacy contact but must not be described as a formally designated Data Protection Officer contact unless that designation has actually been made. These operational safeguards do not constitute a claim of formal PDPA compliance.

Before Clarifold itself collects learner or customer data through accounts, synchronisation, analytics, payments, institutional deployment, or a hosted service, the responsible agent must return to Jerome Queck for a fresh Singapore PDPA review. The review must identify the operating person or entity, applicability and DPO obligations, purposes and consent, access and correction, retention and deletion, processors and overseas transfers, security and breach response, and additional obligations in target jurisdictions. It must update the data-flow inventory and public disclosure before collection starts and must not infer that this ADR resolved future compliance.
