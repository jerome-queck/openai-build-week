# Separate global preferences from contextual understanding

The Learner Model will carry Interaction Preferences such as pace, formality, questioning style, representation, and feedback tendencies across Study Workspaces. These preferences remain revisable and may still vary by topic; global availability does not turn them into fixed learning styles.

Mathematical understanding will remain attached to specific concepts, contexts, and Understanding Evidence. Evidence Transfer may suggest relevant prior evidence in another mission or workspace only after the underlying mathematical concept and conditions have been matched and its provenance remains visible. Cross-context reuse will not silently create a global mastery claim.

The Learner Model will be exposed through a local Learner Model Ledger rather than operating as a hidden profile. Every active Interaction Preference and item of Understanding Evidence used for adaptation will identify its source, date, mathematical context, and relevant correction or exclusion history. The learner may correct or remove an inferred preference and may exclude particular evidence from future adaptation without rewriting the historical Session Record that produced it.

An app-wide Adaptive Reuse Preference will enable provenance-matched cross-mission and cross-workspace adaptation by default. The learner may disable that reuse globally or ignore the Learner Model for one Learning Session. They may also reset the active adaptive model entirely. Disabling, excluding, or resetting stops the corresponding future use but does not silently delete source sessions; deletion of those records remains a separate explicit action.
