# Make delayed transfer learner-opted

Understanding Evidence will normally be elicited through brief, contextual, and skippable Understanding Checks inside the Learning Session. These checks will test explanation, proof reconstruction, error diagnosis, method selection, or application to a fresh example rather than pure recall of a theorem statement or reproduction of the source solution.

When the learner gives a Session Target the Addressed disposition during consolidation, the app may offer `Check me later`. Deferred, Unresolved, and merely Paused work do not trigger the offer. The default choice is no delayed follow-up. If the learner opts in, the app proposes a seven-day Delayed Transfer Check and lets the learner choose another delay. Only explicitly selected targets can create scheduled checks.

A Delayed Transfer Check will use an unseen, reasonably comparable problem requiring the same underlying concept or proof method. It will not simply repeat the original question or change superficial notation. Skipping or dismissing a due check records an absence of delayed evidence, not a failure or loss of previously demonstrated understanding.

Completing a Delayed Transfer Check automatically creates Delayed Transfer Evidence in the Learner Model. This evidence is stronger than a comparable same-session observation because it combines delay with a fresh task, but it remains concept-specific, timestamped, and provenance-backed rather than becoming a blanket mastery label. Both demonstrated understanding and an exposed difficulty may guide later Teaching Moves. Declining, postponing, dismissing, or skipping the check creates no negative evidence.

When a completed check exposes a specific difficulty, its Delayed Check Result will show a compact diagnosis and offer `Start refresher session`. Accepting creates a linked Refresher Session at the relevant Source Anchor or Learning Trail point. The app will not reopen the original session automatically, label the whole topic failed, or require remediation before other work.

Scheduled checks must not silently turn the main dashboard into an assignment backlog. The dashboard will show at most one non-blocking Follow-ups Card containing the aggregate number ready. Opening it reveals a separate optional Follow-up Queue; individual checks will not create individual dashboard cards. Follow-ups never block starting, resuming, consolidating, or completing other work.

Controlled product evaluations may deliberately assign delayed near-transfer tasks to consenting study participants. That research protocol is separate from the ordinary product default and does not authorize automatic follow-ups for learners.
