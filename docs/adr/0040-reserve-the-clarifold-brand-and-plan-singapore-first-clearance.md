# Reserve the Clarifold brand and plan Singapore-first clearance

Clarifold is being created and initially developed in Singapore with an intended global audience and possible future commercial operation. Jerome Queck is the current sole owner. Repository legal notices will state that `Clarifold` and its logo are marks of Jerome Queck and that the software license does not grant permission to present a fork, derivative, or redistributed modified build as the official Clarifold product. Reasonable descriptive references to origin, such as “forked from Clarifold,” remain allowed, but modified distributions must use a different product name and icon and must not imply endorsement. Clarifold will not use the registered-trade-mark symbol unless the relevant registration has succeeded; any use of `™` will be limited and factual.

The public-repository sweep will preserve dated evidence of brand use through repository history and future curated releases, but it will not represent the name as registered or globally cleared. A preliminary web search found unrelated uses of “Clarifold,” including pharmaceutical and travel-mirror products. Those results do not establish a conflict in software or education classes, but they make clear that ordinary web searching is not a legal clearance opinion.

Before serious commercial promotion, paid distribution, material investment in the brand, or onboarding collaborators who will represent the product under the Clarifold name, the project must trigger a Singapore-first trade-mark review. That review must search IPOS for identical and confusingly similar marks, select the relevant goods and services classes, decide whether the applicant should be Jerome Queck or a Singapore entity, and evaluate filing timing. Because the intended market is global, it must also identify priority deadlines and target jurisdictions and decide whether direct national filings or an international route is justified. Registration expense and professional advice are deferred until that gate; repository naming, domain ownership, and use of `™` must not be treated as substitutes for registration.

The PolyForm Noncommercial software license and the Clarifold brand boundary serve different purposes. The former controls permitted use of Jerome Queck's original software; the latter protects product identity and prevents confusion about official builds, endorsement, and origin. Commercial permission for the software does not automatically include a brand license. Any future commercial agreement must address software and brand permissions separately.
