# Expose honest mathematical provenance and verification

Substantive mathematical claims and Learning Artifacts will separate Claim Origin from Verification Level. Claim Origin records whether the exact claim or revision came from the learner, a supplied source, a model, or a mixture of these. Verification Level independently records Not independently checked, Independently checked, or Formally verified. A model-generated claim can therefore be formally verified, while a learner- or source-originated claim can remain not independently checked. Neither dimension is a correctness guarantee.

The learner-facing interface will present a compact combined provenance-and-verification indicator with expandable supporting evidence rather than cluttering every mathematical sentence. Model-generated is an origin, not the lowest verification state. Formally verified may be shown as the current Verification Level only for the exact formal statement accepted by an identified proof checker under a recorded verification environment.

Familiar, well-documented textbook proofs will normally receive a Source-grounded check plus an independent review of whether the reformulation preserves assumptions and logical continuity. Verification will escalate automatically when observable risk rises, including long dependency chains, non-trivial or advanced arguments, sparse or conflicting sources, checker disagreement, or a teaching approach that materially departs from the known proof. A model's stated confidence may contribute to this decision but cannot trigger or waive escalation by itself. Escalated verification will use the most appropriate available device, including Lean when the relevant statement and dependencies can be formalized; work that a formal checker has not accepted cannot receive the Formally verified status.

Failed, inconclusive, or incomplete checking will create a visible Verification Gap identifying the unresolved step, the reason it remains unresolved, and the conclusions that depend on it. A load-bearing gap prevents the dependent conclusion from being presented as settled while the system attempts a repair or a source-grounded alternative. A non-load-bearing gap may allow teaching to continue at the lower honest Verification Level. Checker failure alone will not be presented as evidence that the mathematical claim is false.

Verification evidence is bound to the exact claim revision it examined and carries Verification Currency. A semantic edit or Section Regeneration marks prior evidence for only the affected claims as Changed since check, returns the current claim to Not independently checked unless other current evidence applies, and offers rechecking. The prior evidence remains inspectable but cannot be asserted as current. Unaffected claims retain their Current evidence, while formatting-only changes that preserve the mathematical statement do not invalidate it.
